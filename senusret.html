Senusret III: A Powerful Pharaoh of the Middle Kingdom

Basic Information

  • Dynasty: 12th Dynasty
  • Reign: 1878–1839 BCE
  • Capital: Itjtawy

Achievements

  • Led military campaigns that secured Egypt’s borders and brought stability.
  • Reduced the power of regional rulers, strengthening central authority.
  • Revived craftwork, trade, and urban development.
  • Deified during his lifetime and honored with a cult.

Family

  • Father: Senusret II
  • Mother: Khenemetneferhedjet I Weret
  • Known Wives: Itakayt, Khenemetneferhedjet II, Neferthenut
  • Possible Son: Amenemhat III
  • Possible Daughters: Sithathor, Menet, Senetsenebtysy, Meret

Royal Statuary

Senusret III's statues are distinctive, depicting him at different ages with a somber expression. His head shows exaggerated realism, with hollow eye sockets, prominent ears, and deep lines, while his body remains youthful and muscular.

Pyramid of Senusret III

Located in Dahshur, the pyramid of Senusret III was built with a mudbrick core and limestone casing. It was excavated in 1894 by Jacques de Morgan, who discovered an ancient robbers’ tunnel leading to the burial chamber.

The First Water Canal

Senusret III initiated the first canal linking the Red Sea and the Mediterranean through the Nile, known as the Sesostris Canal. This greatly enhanced trade with the land of Punt and Mediterranean islands such as Crete and Cyprus, also benefiting Egyptian agriculture.
