Mentuhotep II: The Reunifier of Egypt

Basic Information

  • Dynasty: 11th Dynasty
  • Reign: c. 2061–2010 BCE (approx. 51 years)
  • Capital: Thebes (modern Luxor)

Achievements

  • Reunited Egypt after the First Intermediate Period.
  • Defeated the Herakleopolitan kings, restoring centralized rule.
  • Strengthened Thebes as a major religious and political center.
  • Built the mortuary complex at Deir el-Bahari, influencing later architectural designs.
  • Promoted the concept of divine kingship, adopting the title "Son of Hathor."

Relief of Nebhepetre Mentuhotep II and the Goddess Hathor

Artifact Details

  • Period: Middle Kingdom
  • Dynasty: 11th Dynasty
  • Reign: Late reign of Mentuhotep II
  • Date: ca. 2010–2000 B.C.
  • Geography: From Egypt, Upper Egypt, Thebes, Deir el-Bahri, Temple of Mentuhotep II
  • Medium: Limestone, paint

King Nebhepetre Mentuhotep II was revered by the Egyptians as the ruler who reunited Egypt after the era of disunity (the First Intermediate Period) that followed the end of the Old Kingdom. Descended from a family of Theban rulers, the king built his tomb and mortuary temple at Deir el-Bahri in western Thebes. This relief was originally part of the decoration of the temple's main sanctuary that was added to the building at the end of the king's reign.

The fine balance between figures and inscriptions on this block, as well as the clear outline and regular proportions of the king's image with its individualized facial features, exemplify the peak of relief art that had developed over the decades while the vast temple complex was built and decorated. The figure of the goddess Hathor on the right of the block was chiselled away during the Amarna period, when King Akhenaten propagated the sole worship of the god Aten. Hathor was repaired in plaster in early Dynasty 19, and some of the paint on the whole block may also have been renewed at the time.
